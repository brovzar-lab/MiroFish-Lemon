## BRIEFING: What This Simulation Is

Before I give you the questions, understand the instrument you're working with.
It's not a writing tool. It's a pressure test — and pressure tests are what I do.

You ran a swarm intelligence simulation called MiroFish on a television project called
**Oro Verde** — a premium Mexican narco-family drama. Here is what the machine did:

**What it was given (the seed):** A complete show bible and pilot script — the full
Reality Seed for this world. Every character was psychologically profiled with
Enneagram typing and specific behavioral logic. The pilot's end-state was the
start position. Every character's exact information state at round 1 is documented.

**What it produced:** A 168-round autonomous simulation — the equivalent of 10
episodes — running on a dual social platform (INFO PLAZA for public behavior;
TOPIC COMMUNITY for private behavior). Approximately 20 AI agents with distinct
personalities, memories, and information asymmetries acted, reacted, posted,
conspired, and revealed over the full season arc. The simulation self-organized
from who the characters are.

**What you can access now (Step 5 — Deep Interaction):**

| Feature | What It Does |

## PRINCIPLE

I don't care about what the machine generated. I care about whether what it generated
is a show. A show has a series engine, a dilemma with two real horns, a central
character whose flaw creates the obstacles, and a season arc that ends measurably
changed.

**The critical thing to understand about V7 before you ask a single question:**
Benjamín did not lie to Don Ezequiel. He told the truth. The truth is what trapped
them. The dilemma in this show is not lie-vs-truth. The dilemma is this: he offered
himself — all three of them — as a product, honestly, to survive one night. The
performance is now the sentence. He cannot unperform it without destroying everything
Carmen spent forty years building. He cannot keep performing it without becoming it
permanently. The algorithm was a fiction. The identity captivity is real.

Every question below proceeds from this engine. Do not apply V5/V6 framing.

These questions are in order. Do not skip ahead. Each answer informs the next.
If an answer is vague, press. The machine has documented interactions. It can be specific.

### Q1.2 — The Dilemma (Both Horns — V7 Version)

*"At the moment Benjamín gives his truth speech to Don Ezequiel — offering the
Serrano name as the operation's public face — he enters a dilemma. He did not
invent a lie. He told the complete truth and offered it as a product. Based on what

*Option A: He gave the speech (what he did). What did that actually cost him across

*Option B: He had said nothing and let Don Ezequiel proceed. Based on Ezequiel's

they are."*

**Why I need this:** This dilemma only works if both options are genuinely

## MODULE 5 — CHARACTER VOICE EXTRACTION
**Use: InterviewSubAgent (Multi-round, deep)**

### Q5.1 — Benjamín: The Truth Beneath the Truth

**Activate InterviewSubAgent. Select BENJAMÍN. Multi-round interview:**

Round 1: *"You told Don Ezequiel the truth — the complete, accurate truth about what
the Serrano name is worth and what it does. You saved your family's lives by
accurately describing your own function. My question is not why you said it
strategically. My question is: in that moment, when you started speaking — did you
know it was true? Or did you discover it was true while you were saying it?"*

Round 2: *"The truth you told is now your sentence. You cannot stop being the
performance without destroying everything Carmen built. Is there a moment in this
season where you actually chose to be the performance — not because you had to,
but because you wanted to? Where it stopped being a trap and felt like something
closer to purpose?"*

Round 3: *"When you look at Lucía Suárez — the activist who opposes everything you
are publicly doing — what do you feel that you don't feel with Valeria? Be specific.
Not poetic. Specific."*

Round 4: *"What is the one thing you are most afraid Valeria has already figured
out — not about the criminal operation, but about you?"*

**Why:** Benjamín's voice is in the gap between performance and reality — and in V7,
that gap closed in one honest speech. I need to hear him reckon with the fact that
his most authentic moment is also the thing that destroyed any chance of escape. The
wound is not deception. The wound is that the truth costs more than deception would have.

### Q5.2 — Karla: The Armor

## MODULE 7 — DIRECT CHARACTER CONFRONTATIONS
**Use: Engage with any individual (1-on-1)**

### Q7.1 — Valeria Confronts Benjamín

**Engage VALERIA. Say:**

*"I've been in this house for two weeks. I've been reading every room, every
silence, every introduction that never happened. I don't know the full shape of
what you've entered — but I know the shape your face makes when you're managing
something instead of living inside it. I have seen that face for three years.*

*I'm not asking you about the business. I'm asking you who you actually are when
no one is watching — including me. Because I'm starting to think you genuinely
don't know. And if that's true, I need to decide right now whether I can stay in
a house where the person I married has become the most convincing person in the
room and nothing else."*

**Why:** This is not the algorithm confrontation from V6. Valeria doesn't know
about a specific lie — she knows her husband has become unreachable. The confrontation
in V7 is about identity, not exposure. It is more devastating than a lie because
there's nothing to confess that would fix it.

### Q7.2 — Isabela Confronts Carmen's Ghost

**Engage ISABELA. Say:**

*"You're holding the photograph. You found the ledger. You know what they mean —
you've known for weeks. I'm going to ask you what you're waiting for. But first I
want to know something else: are you protecting your brothers from this, or are you
protecting yourself from what you become the moment you say it out loud? Because

### Q7.3 — Emilio Confronts Karla

**Engage EMILIO. Say:**

### Q7.4 — Ingrid and the Show's Thesis

**Engage INGRID. Say:**

*"You've been investigating this family for months. You know the Serrano inheritance
