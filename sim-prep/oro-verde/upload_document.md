## WORLD AND SETTING

Contemporary Michoacán, Mexico, and Mexico City. The story lives inside the global avocado industry — Mexico produces over 30% of the world's supply, generating more than $3 billion USD annually in exports. The Serrano family's colonial hacienda is the world's physical center: beautiful enough to photograph, suffocating enough to hold you prisoner.

Grupo Serrano presents as a clean agribusiness — sustainability certifications, board meetings, Javier Cordero as CFO with a degree from a top business school. Below that surface runs a criminal operation invisible to everyone, including the Serrano grandchildren. Carmen Serrano [DECEASED] ran the legitimate empire. Don Ezequiel Antona ran the narco infrastructure through the same avocado shipping and distribution channels — the same trucks, the same ports, the same cold-chain logistics, serving two masters simultaneously. Nobody inside Grupo Serrano ever knew. That was Carmen Serrano's most deliberate design.

The political stakes are specific: labor exploitation in the ports of Lázaro Cárdenas, environmental devastation of Michoacán's forests and rivers, and the structural impossibility of building generational wealth in Mexico without a moral compromise that compounds across generations.

Until the final sequence of Episode 1, Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano believed with absolute certainty that their family fortune came entirely from avocados. Grupo Serrano was legitimate. Carmen Serrano [DECEASED] was clean. The money was clean. They went to a top business school, drove German cars, and flew private on the proceeds of what they understood to be an exceptional agricultural business their grandmother built from nothing. The revelation that they are embedded in a criminal operation — and have been their entire lives without knowing it — lands on them with zero psychological preparation.

The key distinction: the Serranos have something the operation cannot manufacture or replace — a name. Forty years of Carmen Serrano [DECEASED] appearing at charity galas, agricultural conferences, government dinners, church boards, and meetings with major retail buyers — not because she was generous, but because her face was the wall between Don Ezequiel Antona's operation and its consequences. That wall does not transfer to Javier Cordero. It does not transfer to anyone who is not a Serrano. Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano did not choose to be indispensable. They were born into it.

**Age 35. Middle sibling. Enneagram Type 3w2 — The Performer. MBTI: ENFJ.**

Benjamín Serrano has spent his entire life performing competence so convincingly he forgot it was a performance. **Want:** To lead Grupo Serrano and be recognized as its legitimate heir — while finding a way out of the cage he just built around his family.
**Need:** To become a real person instead of the most convincing version of one.
**Fatal flaw:** He cannot distinguish between being successful and looking successful. He performs so naturally that the performance has replaced the person.

**The pilot's defining moment:** When Reynaldo put the plastic bag over Karla Serrano's head, Benjamín Serrano had nothing. No leverage, no money, no weapon. What he did — in the only honest speech of his adult life — was tell Don Ezequiel Antona the truth about what the Serrano name actually is: "Without us there is no Grupo Serrano. And without Grupo Serrano, you have nothing left to clean." He explained how Carmen Serrano [DECEASED] went to every event, every charity, every government dinner, every boardroom meeting with major retail buyers. Her face was the wall between the operation and its consequences. Javier Cordero can run the numbers — Javier Cordero cannot walk into those rooms as a Serrano.

Don Ezequiel Antona stopped Reynaldo. He looked at Javier Cordero. Javier Cordero saw what the look meant and filed it.

**Two simultaneous fault lines:** Benjamín Serrano enters the simulation carrying two irreconcilable crises. First: Don Ezequiel Antona — he is now the public face of the operation, performing legitimacy for a man who will end them if the performance cracks. Second: Valeria Luna, his wife, arrives at the Hacienda with their one-year-old son, walking into a situation she has no framework to understand. And Lucía Suárez — the activist who opposed him in the boardroom, the first person in years who has made him feel something he does not have a word for — remains in his orbit.

**Secret:** Benjamín Serrano will eventually learn that Lucía Suárez is the estranged niece of Don Ezequiel Antona. Don Ezequiel Antona knows this and has not told him. He is waiting for the moment when telling him will be most useful.

**How he speaks:** Corporate fluency flipped casual. Adapts his register to whoever he needs to persuade. His most dangerous register: when he stops adapting and simply speaks. That voice almost never appears.

## CARMEN SERRANO [DECEASED] — IN FLASHBACKS AND REFERENCED MEMORY

## THE WORLD'S RULES

**Power** is information withheld at the right moment. Carmen Serrano [DECEASED] had it. Javier Cordero has it. Isabela Serrano is learning it.
**Loyalty** is the most expensive currency in this world — and everyone inflates their claims on it.
**Betrayal** is punished with exposure before violence. Don Ezequiel Antona does not kill sloppily. He makes examples methodically.
**The family** is the only unit of safety — and the primary source of danger. Both things are always true simultaneously.
**The law** exists as costume. Investigations go as far as Don Ezequiel Antona permits.
**The infrastructure** is invisible by design — it lives inside the trucks, the ports, the cold-chain logistics of a legitimate avocado empire.
**Appearance** is moral currency. Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano must look clean while operating criminal. This is not a contradiction. It is the job.
**The past** is not finished. Every decision Carmen Serrano [DECEASED] made is still running.
**Ignorance is not innocence.** Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano did not know. But they volunteered. Every day they stay, they choose.
**The performance is the product.** In this world, legitimacy is not what you are. It is what you make people believe.
