# ORO VERDE — Reality Seed
## Simulation Requirement for MiroFish

Carmen Serrano — the matriarch of a billion-dollar Mexican avocado empire — has died, and in her final week, she removed her three grandchildren from her will. The night of her death, Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano hired Reynaldo to recover the disinheritance document from their family lawyer Ernesto Vega's safe. Ernesto Vega came home. He died accidentally. And facing Don Ezequiel Antona — the criminal operator who co-built the empire with Carmen Serrano — the middle sibling Benjamín Serrano gave a ninety-second speech that was entirely, devastatingly true: the Serrano name is the only thing keeping the operation invisible, and without Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano performing legitimacy, the entire infrastructure collapses. Don Ezequiel Antona accepted the offer. Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano are now the public face of an operation they did not choose, did not know existed, and cannot escape without destroying everything.

Three worlds are now in active collision. The legitimate world — investors, sustainability journalists, multinational buyers, government contacts — sees Grupo Serrano's new leadership and believes the family is clean. The investigation world — journalist Ingrid Cervantes, who knows Ernesto Vega's death was not a burglary, and his grieving son Emilio Vega, who has stayed in Michoacán longer than his return flight — is closing in from the outside without knowing Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano are connected. And the criminal world — Don Ezequiel Antona, his embedded CFO Javier Cordero, and Reynaldo somewhere with a blue folder containing Carmen Serrano's full forty-year archive — is watching every move from the inside.

Meanwhile, Lucía Suárez — the environmental activist who is the only person who makes Benjamín Serrano feel something real — is secretly Don Ezequiel Antona's estranged niece, a weapon he has not yet deployed. Valeria Luna, Benjamín Serrano's wife, arrives at the Hacienda with their infant son and no idea what she is walking into. Gabriela Ruiz, the housekeeper who served Carmen Serrano for forty years, holds a secret that could rewrite everything: the plane crash that killed Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano's parents was not an accident — it was a message sent during a territorial war, and Carmen Serrano knew within days and never told anyone.

Simulate how these characters interact, conflict, and collide. Let Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, Isabela Serrano, Don Ezequiel Antona, Javier Cordero, Ingrid Cervantes, Emilio Vega, Lucía Suárez, Valeria Luna, Gabriela Ruiz, Reynaldo, and Padre Mendoza behave from who they are, not from what the story needs them to do. The most interesting questions: What is Javier Cordero actually building on his parallel track? Does Reynaldo ever make contact — and if so, who finds him first? Does the truth about the 2001 plane crash reach Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano before Ingrid Cervantes' investigation does — or at the same moment? Which sibling breaks first, and in which direction? When does Don Ezequiel Antona deploy the Lucía Suárez connection — and what does it destroy?

At the end of the simulation, generate a report with four sections: "The Identity Engine" — how the performance evolved or fractured for each of Benjamín Serrano, Karla Serrano, and Isabela Serrano individually. "The Convergence" — how Ingrid Cervantes and Emilio Vega's external investigations progressed and what they triggered. "The Ghost of Carmen" — how Carmen Serrano's buried decisions moved through the world via Gabriela Ruiz, Padre Mendoza, the blue folder, and the 1982 photograph. "The Asymmetry" — how Don Ezequiel Antona's control held or cracked, what Javier Cordero built in the shadows, and whether Reynaldo was ever activated. Include the simulation's own emergent surprises — alliances, betrayals, and revelations that were not predictable from the starting positions.
