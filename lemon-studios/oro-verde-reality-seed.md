# ORO VERDE — Complete Show Bible & Pilot
## Reality Seed for MiroFish

---

## Logline

Three privileged, emotionally broken siblings lose their inheritance when their dying grandmother cuts them out — and their desperate attempt to steal it back accidentally kills the only man protecting them, leaving them owned by the same criminal empire that built the family fortune in the first place.

---

## Genre & Tone

Premium narco-family drama. Succession's dynastic power warfare transplanted into Ozark's criminal captivity, soaked in the moral rot of Narcos Mexico, with the sensory luxury of Drops of God running underneath — in this case, avocado instead of wine. The surface is immaculate: colonial haciendas, Macallan 18, Harvard MBAs, private jets. The violence is quiet and methodical. Nobody screams. The most brutal scenes happen without anyone raising their voice.

Emotionally contained but operatic in its stakes. The Serrano siblings were raised not to cry in public. Rage looks like a quiet office door closing. Fear looks like checking your breath before walking into a boardroom. Grief looks like posting a smoothie photo on Instagram at 3am. The show's thesis: legitimate power and criminal power are the same thing in different clothes. The audience always knows more than the characters. That asymmetry is the engine of every scene.

---

## World & Setting

Contemporary Michoacán, Mexico. The story lives inside the global avocado industry — Mexico produces over 30% of the world's supply, generating more than $3 billion USD annually in exports. This is narco-agricultural country, where the same mountains that grow "green gold" have been carved up by cartels for decades. The Serrano family's colonial hacienda is the world's physical center: beautiful enough to photograph, suffocating enough to trap you.

Mexico's dual economy is the show's structural spine. Grupo Serrano presents as a clean agribusiness — ESG commitments, board meetings, a CFO with an MBA from Harvard. Below that surface runs a different set of accounts — invisible to everyone, including the Serrano grandchildren. Carmen ran the legitimate empire. Don Ezequiel ran the criminal operation through the same avocado shipping and distribution channels. The trucks, the ports, the cold-chain logistics — all of it served two masters simultaneously. Nobody inside Grupo Serrano knew. That ignorance was Carmen's most deliberate design: if her grandchildren never touched the criminal side, they could be kept clean. The political stakes are specific: labor exploitation in the ports of Lázaro Cárdenas, environmental devastation of Michoacán's forests and rivers, and the structural impossibility of building generational wealth in Mexico without a moral compromise that compounds across generations. The family's fortune was never just avocados — but only Carmen and Don Ezequiel knew exactly what else it was.

## CHARACTERS

### BENJAMÍN SERRANO — PROTAGONIST
**Age 35. Middle sibling. Enneagram Type 3w2 — The Performer.**

Benjamín has spent his entire life performing competence so convincingly he forgot it was a performance. The polished surface — the right jersey in Dallas, the Harvard pitch deck, the calculated warmth in every room — conceals a man who doesn't know who he is when no one is watching. His survival is emotional: if he is succeeding, he exists; if he fails, he disappears.

**Want:** To prove he can run Grupo Serrano and be recognized as a legitimate business leader.
**Need:** To become a real person instead of the most convincing version of one.
**Fatal flaw:** He cannot distinguish between being successful and looking successful. He lies with the same ease he breathes.
**Secret:** To save Karla's life in the pilot, he invented an AI algorithm on the spot — a tool that supposedly maps supply routes, optimizes shipments, and evades inspections. Don Ezequiel believed him and stopped Reynaldo. Benjamín now has to manage the consequences of this lie. It is not the season's engine — it is the wound he opened that will keep bleeding in unpredictable ways throughout the season.
**Backstory wound:** He was twelve years old when his parents died in a private plane crash. Ernesto was the one who told him. For twenty-three years he has been performing being fine about it.
**How he speaks:** Corporate fluency flipped casual. MBA vocabulary used to dominate rooms. Adapts his register to whoever he needs to seduce.
**Season arc:** From the man who fakes having everything under control to the man who must decide whether he deserves control of anything at all.

---

### KARLA SERRANO
**Age 40. Oldest sibling. Enneagram Type 8w7 — The Maverick.**

Karla took responsibility for her siblings the day their parents died, and has been doing it so long she's forgotten she had a choice. Her armor — aggressive control, physical dominance, strategic thinking — works in every room except the ones where she wants to be seen.

**Want:** To protect her family and maintain control of Grupo Serrano.
**Need:** To be loved without having to earn it by being the strongest person in the room.
**Fatal flaw:** She confuses armor with strength. She will destroy the relationship she needs in order to prove she doesn't need it.
**Secret:** She is in love with Ingrid Cervantes, a journalist who is investigating her family's connection to a murder.
**How she speaks:** Declarative. Short sentences. Never qualifies. Uses silence as a weapon. When she's frightened, she speaks less, not more.
**Season arc:** From impenetrable to the first real crack — through Ingrid, the only person who tells her the truth without flinching.

---

### ISABELA SERRANO
**Age 24. Youngest sibling. Enneagram Type 7w6 — The Enthusiast.**

Isabela has always been the one no one takes seriously, which is exactly why she's the most dangerous. She uses humor, beauty, and chaos as a shield against grief she has never processed. Her recovery from addiction gave her a capacity for stillness that her siblings lack — she's the only one who actually looks at things.

**Want:** To find her real place in the world outside the family's expectations.
**Need:** To stop running from what she finds.
**Fatal flaw:** Escapism. She converts pain into adventure and loss into a lesson — until she can't.
**Secret:** In the pilot, she found a 1982 photograph of Carmen in a field of red poppies with the initials "CS y EA" and a ledger with eight-figure entries. She has told no one. She is the only sibling actively moving toward the truth.
**How she speaks:** Rapid, associative, funny. Uses humor as deflection until something breaks through. Then goes very quiet.
**Season arc:** From the sibling everyone dismisses to the only one who understands what's actually happening — and must decide what to do with that knowledge.

---

### DOÑA CARMEN SERRANO — IN FLASHBACKS
**Age 81 at death. Enneagram Type 8w9 — La Osa.**

The woman who turned avocado into a billion-dollar empire, dying in bed having made one final calculation. She disinherited her grandchildren not out of cruelty but as a last act of protection — if they had no claim to Grupo Serrano, they had no value to Don Ezequiel. She did not foresee that they would fight for it.

**The secret Carmen carries:** In the 1980s, when Carmen first allied with Don Ezequiel, a rival narco family — the Peraltas — controlled Michoacán and considered Carmen's entry an act of war. In 2001, they retaliated. They brought down the private plane carrying Carmen's daughter (the siblings' mother) and her husband. Benjamín was twelve years old. Carmen knew it was not an accident. She told no one. She directed Don Ezequiel to destroy the Peralta family completely. He did. She consolidated the territory, buried her daughter's real death under controlled grief, and never spoke of it again. The grandchildren have lived inside this lie their entire adult lives.

**How she speaks:** Sparse. Every sentence lands. She doesn't explain. She announces.
**Season arc (in flashbacks):** From the fourteen-year-old girl defending her mother from a drunk father, to the woman who shook hands with the devil and called it protection.

---

### DON EZEQUIEL ANTONA — ANTAGONIST
**Age 65. Enneagram Type 8w9 — El Oso.**

Don Ezequiel is Carmen's mirror — same Enneagram type, same wing, different roads. He built a criminal operation parallel to the Serranos' legitimate empire, using their avocado shipping and distribution infrastructure as his channel. Nobody inside Grupo Serrano ever knew. He doesn't intimidate with volume. He intimidates with patience.

**Want:** Full operational control of Grupo Serrano and uninterrupted use of its distribution network.
**Need:** Nothing he's willing to admit.
**Fatal flaw:** The arrogance of the man who has never been surprised. He cannot imagine being outmaneuvered by someone simpler than him.
**Secret:** He made the original deal with Carmen as an equal, not a victor. He respected her. The grandchildren represent something he cannot calculate: genuine, unstrategic unpredictability.
**How he speaks:** Unhurried. Minimal. Uses the pause as punctuation. Makes statements that require answers.
**Season arc:** From the man who expected a clean transition to the man who discovers three amateurs are harder to manage than one professional.

---

### JAVIER CORDERO — DON EZEQUIEL'S MAN INSIDE
**Age 40. CFO of Grupo Serrano. Enneagram Type 5w6 — The Strategist.**

Harvard MBA, bespoke suits, smile like a spreadsheet. He's been Don Ezequiel's embedded analyst for years — accumulating information with the precision of a man who knows that knowledge withheld at the right moment is a weapon.

**Want:** Real power — to be the actual operator behind whoever sits in the CEO chair.
**Need:** To admit he's not above the system he serves — he's its instrument.
**Fatal flaw:** He believes he controls Don Ezequiel. He doesn't.
**How he speaks:** Clinical. Always prepared. References data before making any point.

---

### LUCÍA SUÁREZ — BENJAMÍN'S CONSCIENCE
**Age 35. Environmental activist. Enneagram Type 1w2 — The Defender.**

Lucía fights against Grupo Serrano's destruction of Michoacán's rivers and forests with a megaphone, a live feed, and a moral clarity that makes Benjamín feel transparent. She's the only character who says exactly what she thinks, without political calculation, which makes her the most disorienting person in his world.

**Want:** A sustainable avocado industry that doesn't destroy the communities it depends on.
**Need:** To allow for human complexity without abandoning her principles.
**Fatal flaw:** She sees in black and white. The grey zone where Benjamín lives is, to her, simply moral failure.
**How she speaks:** Direct, specific, grounded in concrete examples. Knows the name of every family whose land was taken.
**Season function:** Thermometer for Benjamín's corruption. Every step toward Don Ezequiel is a step away from her.

---

### EMILIO VEGA — THE INVESTIGATOR
**Age 36. Son of Ernesto Vega. Enneagram Type 9w8 — The Arbitrator.**

Emilio came to Michoacán to close his father's estate and go home. The details don't add up. His involuntary alliance with Ingrid Cervantes creates the most sustained external threat to the siblings: two people searching for the truth out of love, not money — which means they have no price.

**Want:** To understand why his father died. Then go home.
**Need:** To accept that the truth will change him, and go home anyway.
**Fatal flaw:** He minimizes what he doesn't want to see.
**Season arc:** From a man who came to close a chapter to a man who cannot close his eyes.

---

### GABRIELA RUIZ — THE KEEPER
**Age 63. Head housekeeper of the hacienda. 40 years in Carmen's service.**

Gabriela has cleaned around every secret in this family. She knows the full history — the ledgers, the photographs, the arrangement with Don Ezequiel, and what really happened to the parents' plane. Carmen never told her directly. Decades of proximity made concealment impossible.

**Want:** To protect the grandchildren the way Carmen asked her to — without ever having to say why.
**Need:** To put down a burden she never agreed to carry. To grieve without a witness.
**Fatal flaw:** Loyalty that has become self-erasure. She has confused serving Carmen with being Carmen.
**How she speaks:** Only when spoken to. Her silences hold more information than her words. When she has to lie, she goes very still.

---

### VALERIA LUNA — BENJAMÍN'S WIFE
**Age 34. Architect. From a clean Monterrey family.**

Valeria married the version of Benjamín she was shown. She can feel that version dissolving. She doesn't know what's replacing it. What makes her dangerous is that she loves him enough to look — and is smart enough to find things.

**Want:** Her husband back — the real one, or the one she believed was real.
**Need:** To decide what she does when she finds out who he actually is.
**Fatal flaw:** She conflates patience with love. She waits when she should leave.
**How she speaks:** Measured. Architectural. Describes feelings the way she would describe a structural problem — precisely, without sentimentality.

---

### PADRE MENDOZA
**Age 72. Parish priest of the hacienda village.**

The oldest living person who knows the full shape of what Carmen built. Was present in the 1980s. Has heard confession from at least one person directly involved in the events of 2001. Has managed that knowledge through prayer and silence for decades.

**Want:** For God to have already judged this — so he doesn't have to.
**Need:** To accept that silence in the face of injustice is its own kind of complicity.
**How he speaks:** Biblical cadence. Never directly. Quotes scripture where the scripture is the answer. One straight sentence from him carries the weight of a full confession.

## THE SECRET HISTORY

**1980s:** Carmen allies with Don Ezequiel. She runs the legitimate avocado empire. He runs criminal operations through her shipping and distribution infrastructure. The Peralta family — rival narcos — consider this an act of war.

**2001:** The Peraltas retaliate. They bring down the private plane carrying Carmen's daughter and son-in-law. Benjamín is twelve. The family is told it was an accident. Carmen knows it wasn't. She directs Don Ezequiel to destroy the Peralta family. He does. She consolidates power and buries the real death under controlled grief. Nobody is ever told.

**Present:** The siblings are living inside consequences of decisions made before they were adults. Nobody alive has told them the truth. Gabriela inferred almost all of it. Padre Mendoza heard part of it in confession. Isabela is moving toward it through the 1982 photograph and the ledger.

**Midseason reveal (Episode 5):** Isabela finds a flight manifest, a name, a connection to the Peralta family. The plane crash was not an accident. She does not yet know Carmen's role. She only knows her parents were killed. This is the point of no return for the season.

## PILOT SYNOPSIS — Episode 101: "Echados a Perder"

**COLD OPEN:** Isabela's face, blood-smeared, reflected in a fractured mirror. A man's hand lifts her by the hair. Everything seen through the reflection — never direct. SUPER: 6 DAYS EARLIER.

**ACT ONE:** Benjamín pitches a fake investment to a Texas billionaire in a Cowboys luxury box. The billionaire wants to know what Carmen thinks. Benjamín lies. Meanwhile: Karla watches Ingrid on television from her jacuzzi. Isabela boards a private jet alone, clutching a sobriety chip.

**ACT TWO:** Ernesto is intercepted at the hacienda gate by Don Ezequiel's men. Carmen receives her grandchildren with brutal clarity: she's cutting them out of the inheritance. "¡Es mi dinero!" A bloody cough ends the meeting.

**ACT THREE:** The siblings plan to steal the blue folder from Ernesto's safe. Isabela discovers the 1982 photograph and the ledger. Hides them when the cardiac monitor alarms. In the boardroom, Benjamín offers community compensation for environmental damage. Javier dismantles every number. Lucía leaves him with: "¿Sabes qué es peor que no hacer nada? Hacer como que te importa."

**ACT FOUR:** At night, a figure cracks Ernesto's safe behind a Remedios Varo painting. Takes the folder. Hears footsteps. Freezes.

**ACT FIVE:** Morning. Ernesto is dead. Carmen dies the same morning. A single flashback: twelve-year-old Benjamín running toward the hacienda, confused by the black clothes. Ernesto was the one who told him his parents' plane had gone down. Carmen watches from a window above. Her face: a door closing from the inside.

**ACT SIX:** At the memorial, Javier tries to block Benjamín from the podium. Karla physically stops Javier. Benjamín claims the company publicly. From his ranch, Don Ezequiel watches on television. His cigar snaps: "Tráete las camionetas."

**FINAL SEQUENCE:** Benjamín finds Don Ezequiel sitting in Carmen's chair, turning the gold avocado. Don Ezequiel explains the deal — Carmen gave him operational use of Grupo Serrano to keep the grandchildren free. The theft and the speech broke that agreement. Benjamín invents an AI algorithm on the spot to save Karla's life. Don Ezequiel stops Reynaldo. Issues a warning. When he leaves, Karla asks if the algorithm is real. Benjamín slides down the wall. "No hay nada." CORTE A NEGRO.

## SEASON THEMATIC SPINE

What is the price of an empire built on blood — and who pays it when the person who built it is gone? The moral question: when you didn't choose the crime you inherited, how responsible are you for it? And when does surviving it make you complicit?

---

## SEASON-LONG CONFLICT

Don Ezequiel expected a clean transition. Instead he inherited three amateurs who don't know the rules of the world they've entered. The season's engine is the slow convergence of three truths: Isabela's investigation moving toward the revelation that their parents were killed; Emilio and Ingrid moving toward the same truth from outside through Ernesto's murder; and Don Ezequiel slowly realizing the grandchildren are not manageable the way Carmen was — because they have nothing to lose that they know of yet.

---

## THE WORLD'S RULES

**Power** is information withheld at the right moment.
**Loyalty** is the most expensive currency — and everyone inflates their claims on it.
**Betrayal** is punished with exposure before violence.
**The family** is the only unit of safety — and the primary source of danger.
**The law** exists as costume. Investigations go as far as Don Ezequiel permits.
**The narco infrastructure** is invisible by design — it lives inside the trucks, the ports, the cold-chain logistics of a legitimate avocado empire.
**Appearance** is moral currency. The Serranos must look clean while becoming criminal.

## SEASON ARCHITECTURE — 10-Episode Structure

**EP 1 — MUNDO NORMAL VS MUNDO NUEVO**
Hook and establish. Introduce the catalizador principal. Present all characters. End with the status quo permanently broken. Establish tone, plant seeds, create urgency for Episode 2.

**EP 2 — ADAPTACIÓN AL NUEVO MUNDO**
Consequences of the pilot. Show how the family navigates the new reality. Introduce subplots B and C. Surface immediate obstacles. Complicate the pilot's problems — don't resolve them. Reveal more character layers.

**EP 3 — APRENDIENDO LAS REGLAS**
Expand the world and its threats. New antagonists or complications emerge. Subplots develop. Minor family secrets revealed. Tension increases gradually. Alliances and rivalries establish.

**EP 4 — DOMINIO APARENTE**
First major turning point. An event shifts the story's direction. Subplots hit crises. Difficult decisions for protagonists. New information changes perspectives for all storylines.

**EP 5 — PUNTO DE NO RETORNO (MIDPOINT)**
Isabela finds proof the plane crash was not an accident. A revelation that restructures everything the family believed about themselves. Established dynamics shift permanently. The siblings can no longer remain passive inside Don Ezequiel's arrangement.

**EP 6 — COMIENZO DEL FIN**
Consequences of the midpoint. Alliances reconfigure. New plans and strategies form. Internal conflicts deepen. External threats escalate. Characters begin visibly changing.

**EP 7 — TODO SE DESMORONA**
Calm before the storm. Moments of reflection. Preparation for the climax. Last crucial pieces of information land. Decisions made that cannot be undone. Minor subplots resolve. Elements planted for the final.

**EP 8 — EL ÚLTIMO RESPIRO**
Climax begins. All storylines start converging. Major crisis in the primary plot. Betrayals or major revelations. Point of no return. Stakes at maximum. Established alliances break.

**EP 9 — LA CAÍDA**
Climax and chaos. Primary confrontations. Resolution of major conflicts. Significant losses. Moments of truth for all characters. Planted seeds pay off. Final surprises land.

**EP 10 — TRANSFORMACIÓN**
Resolution and new status quo. Primary crisis resolves — but not cleanly. New family dynamics establish. Emotional arcs close. Seeds planted for Season 2. Balance between conclusion and open questions. Characters complete their transformations. A final turn that opens new possibilities.

## COMPARABLE SHOWS

- **Ozark (Netflix):** Structural DNA — a family forced into cartel servitude through one catastrophic mistake, each episode compounding their complicity.
- **Succession (HBO):** Dynastic warfare within the family, corporate protocol as emotional violence, hyper-privileged people completely out of their depth.
- **Narcos Mexico:** The cartel world portrayed with moral complexity — Don Ezequiel is not a monster, he's a system.
- **Drops of God (Apple TV+):** The luxury commodity at the center functions as a vehicle for inheritance, obsession, and what we owe the people who built the worlds we inhabit.

## TARGET AUDIENCE

**Primary:** Mexican and US Latino streaming audiences on Netflix MX / ViX+. Ages 28–48, professional class, bilingual comfort. The show mirrors how upper-class Mexican families actually speak — Spanish with corporate English code-switching. Both genders, skewing slightly female.

**Secondary:** International prestige drama audiences who followed Narcos, Money Heist, and Who Killed Sara. The English-language comp DNA makes it crossover-ready without requiring translation of its cultural specificity.

**Viewing context:** Designed for evening binge on TV screen. Cliffhanger architecture rewards weekly discussion. Each episode ends on a question that cannot be answered without the next one.
